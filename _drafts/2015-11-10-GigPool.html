This weeked hackathon at ATDC was set up backwards to other hackathons or startup weekends. Instead of participants trying to solve problems they have identified and see a potential market need, nine startup companies brought the real world challenges they were facing in their business. Whether it was a project that sat on the back burner while they addressed more pressing issues, or a problem they faced in their application execution, the weekend was also meant to serve to distinguish the local talent in developers, designers, and other technology creatives for potential employment.

I decided to join a team for Kanga Technology, an ATDC Signature company, providing a crowdsourced, on-demand local delivery platform. The project the executives pitched was directed at the gig economy, towards startups that provided on-demand jobs and services such as Uber, Lyft, Jobseeker, TaskRabbit, and many others. The problem was that for any potential employee of these companies, a driver for uber, a job worker for TaskRabbit, they would have to go to each of those companies websites and fill out an application with all their information. For someone interested in making a career out of gig economy jobs, this may have to be done six to a dozen times, as

I took charge of designing the backend database and overall function of the application, through setting up a Rails API to interact with the iOS application with JSON. Unfortunately, our iOS team member had to withdraw from the competition on the second day, so I had to convert everything back to a web based application. My two front end team members really stepped up in designing the views for the application, on top of their work on the logo and other design features.

Tech and tools used:
Ruby on Rails, HAML, Javascript, jQuery, Semantic UI, AJAX, OmniAuth, Pocket API, AlchemyAPI, PostgreSQL, pg search
